Dr. Carlos Chagas

Sanitary doctor who discovered the protozoan Trypanosoma Cruzi

Here's a time line of Dr. Carlos Chagas:

  • 1879 - Born in Oliveira, Minas Gerais
  • 1883 - Became a fatherless
  • 1886 - He was sent to Colégio São Luís, in Itu, São Paulo.
  • 1888 - Upon learning of the abolition of slavery, he fled the school, claiming that his mother was in trouble with the farm slaves. After being captured and showing sadness for living away from his family, he was taken back to Minas Gerais.
  • 1897 - Joined the Faculty of Medicine in Rio de Janeiro. Still a student, he became an assistant in the malaria course.
  • 1902 - At the recommendation of professor Miguel Couto, he started working at Institute Manguinhos (now Osvaldo Cruz), under the guidance of Osvaldo Cruz.
  • 1904 - Worked as a clinician at the Jurujuba hospital in Niterói. That same year, he installed his office in Rio de Janeiro.
  • 1905 - Headed a prophylactic campaign against malaria, at the invitation of Company Docas de Santos.
  • 1905 - Back in Rio de Janeiro, joined the Manguinhos team to carry out a campaign to combat malaria in the Baixada Fluminense.
  • 1907 - He was appointed to head a commission designated to fight a malaria epidemic that was spreading among workers installing a train line in the city of Lassance, in Minas Gerais.
  • 1909 - He discovered an insect called a barber that had a habit of stinging the face and sucking people's blood. Upon examining the insect, he found in his intestine a new species of flagellated protozoan, which he later called Trypanosoma cruzi
  • April 22, 1909 - The discovery of the disease, which would later be called Chagas, was published in Magazine Brasil-Médico. In August of that same year, Carlos Chagas published the first volume of the Institute Osvaldo Cruz magazine, a complete study on Chagas disease and the evolutionary cycle of the protozoan that causes the disease.
  • Between 1911 and 1912 - Conducted a complete epidemiological study in 52 cities in the Amazon valley, a region plagued by major epidemics, especially malaria. In his report, Carlos Chagas was indignant at the region's poverty situation.
  • 1917 - After Osvaldo Cruz's death, I took over the direction of Institute Manguinhos.
  • 1918 - Spanish flu arrives in Rio de Janeiro and after 15,000 deaths in the city in two months, Carlos Chagas was called to head the campaign against the epidemic. Within a week, he set up makeshift hospitals and emergency laboratories and mobilized the active part of the population. By the end of the year, the epidemic had been stopped.
  • 1919 - He was appointed by the President of the Republic, Epitácio Pessoa, to head the National Department of Public Health. During this period, he carried out reforms in the rural prophylaxis service, installed inspectorates specialized in combating tuberculosis, syphilis and leprosy. And with the support of the Rockefeller Foundation, Carlos Chagas created the Health Nursing service.
  • 1923 - Created the Anna Neri Nursing School, introducing nursing education in Brazil.
  • 1925 - Was indicated to teach Tropical Medicine at the Faculty of Medicine of Rio de Janeiro.
  • 1926 - Left the National Department, but remained directing Manguinhos.
  • November 8, 1934 - Died at his home in Rio de Janeiro, victim of a heart attack.

“The discovery of this disease is the most beautiful example of the power of logic in the service of science. Never before, in the fields of biological research, had such a complex and brilliant discovery been made and, what more, by a single researcher ”.

-- Oswaldo Cruz
